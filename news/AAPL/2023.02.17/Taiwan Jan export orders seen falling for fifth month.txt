The median forecast from a poll of 11 economists was for export orders to fall by 25.0% from a year earlier. Forecasts ranged for a contraction of between 17% and 31.8%.Taiwan's export orders, a bellwether of global technology demand, fell 23.2% in December. Taiwan's export orders are a leading indicator of demand for high-tech gadgets and Asian exports, and typically lead actual exports by two to three months.The island's manufacturers, including the world's largest contract chipmaker Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co Ltd, are a key part of the global supply chain for technology giants including Apple Inc.The data for January will be released on Monday.
