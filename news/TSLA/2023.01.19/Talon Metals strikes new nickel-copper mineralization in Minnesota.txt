Demand for nickel, a silvery-white metal used to boost energy storage in a battery's cathode, is expected to jump over the next decade as electric vehicles go mainstream. Earlier this year, Tesla Inc signed its first U.S. nickel supply deal with Talon, securing a key U.S. source of the metal for its battery factories in Texas and Nevada.Talon said its Minnesota project, slated to open by 2026, is a joint venture with global miner Rio Tinto.
