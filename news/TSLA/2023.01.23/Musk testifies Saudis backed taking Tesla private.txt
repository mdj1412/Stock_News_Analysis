Musk acknowledged he did not discuss a takeover price with representatives of Saudi Arabia's sovereign wealth fund, the Public Investment Fund, but said they made clear they would do what it took to make a buyout happen. And he said he was "upset" when they later "appeared to be backpedaling."The lawyer for the investors told the court that written evidence does not support Musk's claim that the Saudi fund made a commitment to him, adding that minutes of a meeting between Musk and the Saudis showed they wanted to learn more about Musk's plan.  Investors say they lost millions as a result of Musk's Aug. 7, 2018 tweet saying that he had "funding secured" to take Tesla private at $420 per share.Tesla's stock price surged after Musk's tweets, and later fell as it became clear the buyout would not materialize.
