TOKYO, Feb 3 (Reuters) - Japan's Nikkei share average
ended at a seven-week high on Friday, underpinned by the
Nasdaq's strong performance overnight, with Sony Group and
others leading the charge after reporting strong growth outlook.The Nikkei rose 0.39% to close at 27,509.46, its
highest close since Dec 16. The index rose 0.46% for the week.The broader Topix edged up 0.26% at 1,970.26 but
lost 0.6% for the week."Companies' outlook was clearly reflected in their share
prices, like Sony," said Takamasa Ikeda, senior portfolio
manager at GCI Asset Management."Investors wanted to confirm their outlook before making
active bets."The Nasdaq and S&P 500 ended higher overnight and touched
roughly five-month highs, as a more dovish-than-expected message
from U.S. Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell boosted equities
and Meta Platforms shares soared on rigorous cost controls.Sony Group jumped 6.18% after the game maker raised
profit forecast and raised its PlayStation 5 game console sales
target by 1 million units to 19 million for the year to March.ANA Holdings rose 1.68% after the operator of
Japan's largest airline raised its full-year profit forecast as
travel demand recovers.On the other hand, competitor Japan Airlines
slipped 3.6% even as it returned to profit.Z Holdings surged 12.21% after announcing a merger
with a messaging app company Line Corp and internet firm Yahoo
Japan.Kawasaki Kisen rose 4.49% after the shipping firm
raised full-year dividend forecast, while cutting annual net
profit forecast.Trading houses Marubeni and Itochu <8001.T raised
their annual dividend forecast, thanks to record quarterly
profits, and announced share buybacks to reward investors.Marubeni gained 4.41% while Itochu lost 1.62%.Among losers, Panasonic Holdings fell 3.11% after
the maker of electric appliances cut its annual operating profit
forecast.
